About Corona Virus

Most people infected with the virus develop mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without special treatment. However, some people are seriously ill and need medical attention. Older people and those with underlying conditions such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory disease or cancer are more likely to develop serious illness. Anyone can catch COVID-19 and become seriously ill or die at any age.

Coronavirus what it is?

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-COV-2 virus. Most people with COVID-19 develop mild to moderate symptoms and recover without special treatment. But some patients are seriously ill and need medical treatment.

Wear Mask

All people aged 2 or older should wear masks or protective gear correctly when wearing them on public transport such as planes, trains, buses and ferries and at transport hubs such as airports, railway stations and ports, especially in crowded or poorly ventilated places.

How can people protect themselves and others from COVID-19 in this situation?

Vaccine

Get vaccinated against COVID-19 and get vaccinated on time COVID-19 vaccines are effective in preventing illness. The COVID-19 vaccine is highly effective in preventing severe illness, hospitalization and death. Vaccination is the best way to slow the spread of SARS-COV-2, the virus that causes COVID-19. CDC recommends that everyone who meets vaccination eligibility get their COVID-19 vaccine on time, including those with compromised immune systems.

Mask

All 2 years of age or older (including passengers and crew) on public transportation such as plane, train, bus, ferry and traffic hub, such as airports, railway stations and ports) should be correct when wearing joint degree of face masks or protective mask (breathing apparatus), especially in crowded or poorly ventilated places (such as the airport boarding).

Read More

Social distance

At home: Avoid close contact with sick people if possible. If possible, stay at least 6 feet away from the patient and other family members. If you are caring for a sick person, make sure you wear a well-fitted mask and take other measures to protect yourself. Indoors in public places: If you haven't received your COVID-19 vaccine on time, stay at least 6 feet away from others, especially if you are at high risk of serious COVID-19.
